# **Agentic Community Governance**

## **Introduction and Mission**

The Agentic Community is a collaborative initiative bringing together organizations and individuals dedicated to understanding and addressing the modern challenges of agentic AI. We aim to foster knowledge sharing, develop best practices, and promote responsible innovation in the field of agentic systems.

## **Organizational Structure**

### **Membership Categories**

* **Core Member Organizations**: Companies or institutions that have formally committed resources to the community and its initiatives.
* **Individual Contributors**: Independent experts and practitioners who have demonstrated engagement and contributed meaningfully to the community's objectives.
* **General Community**: Anyone who participates in public discussions, events, and open repositories.

### **Leadership**

* **Steering Committee**: Composed of two representatives from each Core Member Organization and (3) selected Individual Contributors based on merit and engagement in the community.
* **Working Groups**: Topic-specific teams formed to address challenges or projects.
    * **Agent Development**
    * **Agent Interoperability**
    * **Agent Operations**
    * **Agent Security**
* **Community Coordinator(s)**: Responsible for day-to-day operations, communication, and coordination.

## **Membership Process**

### **Core Member Organizations**

Organizations become Core Members through :

* Demonstrated prior commitment to the community
* Dedication of resources (financial, personnel, or technical)
* Approval by majority vote of the existing Steering Committee

### **Individual Contributors**

Individuals (defined as an unaffiliated individual or one whose organization is not a member and is allowing them to participate in an unaffiliated capacity) may be invited to join the steering committee based on:

* Sustained meaningful contributions to community projects
* Active participation in discussions and events
* Demonstrated expertise in relevant domains
* Nomination by a current member and approval by majority vote

## **Decision-Making Process**

### **General Principles**

* Decisions are made by majority vote (\>50%) unless specified otherwise
* Transparency in deliberations is encouraged
* All members should have opportunity to provide input before significant decisions

### **Voting**

* Each Core Member Organization has two votes on the Steering Committee
* Selected Individual Contributors on the Steering Committee each have one vote
* Voting may occur in meetings or through asynchronous digital platforms
* Quorum requires participation from at least 60% of voting members

### **Steering Committee Decisions**

* Decisions affecting:
    * Strategic Direction
    * Community Governance
    * Budget allocation
    * Working Group Proposals
* Voting Requirements
    * Quorum: ⅔ of members
    * Passing Thresholds: \>50% for regular decisions, \>75% for governance changes
    * Tie-breaker: Committee chair/lead

### **Work Group Decisions**

* Decisions affecting:
    * Technical decisions within working group
    * Implementation details / feature prioritization
    * Work allocation
    * Proposals required to go to steering committee
* Voting Requirements
    * Quorum: 50% of active working group members
    * Passing Thresholds: \>50%
    * Tie-breaker: Working Group Leads

### **Community Decisions**

* Decisions affecting:
    * Simple decisions
* Process
    * Document in Github issues/discussions
    * Raise in Meeting Agenda
* Voting Requirements
    * Quorum: 50% of active working group members
    * Passing Thresholds: \>50%

## **Meetings and Communication**

### **Regular Meetings**

* Steering Committee: Monthly meetings
* Working Groups: Every other week and as determined by the groups
* Community-wide: Every other week
* Working Group Leads: Weekly

### **Communication Channels**

* Official announcements via the Agentic Community GitHub and Slack
* Public discussions in Agentic Community/Community GitHub organizations
* Private discussions for sensitive matters via the steering committee email list (steering-committee@agentic-community.com)

## **Contribution Guidelines**

By contributing, members grant relevant licenses to the community

### **Code Contributions**

* All code contributions must adhere to the Apache 2.0 license
* Code submissions follow [GitHub pull request workflow](https://github.com/agentic-community/community/blob/main/CONTRIBUTING.md)
* Technical contributions require review by at least one community member from the working group

### **Research and Documentation**

* Written artifacts are licensed CC-4.0-BY
* Research findings should be shared with the community when possible
* Documentation follows community templates and guidelines
* Intellectual contributions are recognized in publication credits
    * Individuals are credited with an acknowledgement of the Agentic Community
    * An example is provided in the appendix

## **Code of Conduct**

The latest version of the Agentic Community Code of Conduct can be found [here](https://github.com/agentic-community/community/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md)

All participants in the Agentic Community agree to:

* Engage respectfully and professionally
* Provide constructive feedback
* Recognize and honor diverse perspectives
* Avoid discriminatory, harassing, or harmful behavior
* Disclose relevant conflicts of interest
* Respect confidentiality when appropriate
* Follow the Anti-Trust Policy

Any violations should be disclosed to the Code of Conduct email list: [coc@agentic-community.com](mailto:coc@agentic-community.com). ## **Steering Committee**

The Steering Committee meets monthly and may meet on an emergent basis if necessary. The purpose of the steering committee is:

* Conflict resolution between maintainers, contributors, members, and external parties
* Policy decisions (licensing, code of conduct enforcement, etc.)
* Resource allocation decisions
* Community representation and external partnerships

## **Working Groups**

### **Formation**

* Working Groups may be proposed by any member
* Establishment requires approval by Steering Committee majority
* Each Working Group must have a defined charter and objectives

### **Operation**

* Working Groups must have 2 leads to help steer and run the working group
* Working Groups must meet collectively to ensure proper coordination
* Regular progress reports to the Steering Committee

## **Amendment Process**

This governance document may be amended by:

1. Proposal of changes by any Steering Committee member
2. Review period of at least two weeks
3. Approval by a two-thirds majority of the Steering Committee
4. Publication of updated governance document

## **Dissolution**

In the event the community decides to dissolve:

* Decision requires a two-thirds majority of the Steering Committee
* Assets and resources will be appropriately redistributed or archived
* Public notice will be provided at least 30 days before dissolution
